Crime Distribution
Crime Distribution dashboard shows crime distribution over four different factors. 1) Geographic location 2) Geographic location and neighborhoods 3) Time 4) Neighborhood.You can apply different filters to see more specific details. For example, you can select a range of date and types of crime. Crime distribution map shows the geographical distribution of crime incidents in Vancouver. You can zoom in to see a more specific region or zoom out to see overall distribution. You can hover the mouse pointer over crime incident point and it will show you more detailed information about that crime. Crime pie chart shows crime distribution over different neighborhoods. You can hover the mouse over different pie to get more detailed information. With Time-quantity chart you can visualize counts and types of crime over the continuous time period. Neibhour- quantity chart show counts and types of crime over different neighborhoods. Time-quantity and neighborhoods-quantity graphs are linked with each other. If you click any crime type for any specific neighborhood, its corresponding graph will be displayed in time-quantity chart.

Crime and Climate
This dashboard shows the relation between crime and climate. The first chart displays the crime distribution over the map for any given month. You can select or change month using "Month of Date" combo box. Second chart display relation of crime count and temperature over a selected time range. You can change the date range quarterly.

Crime and Events
This dashboard shows the relation between crime and historical events. Panel on the right contains the list of all important historical events from 2003 till 2017. On the left side, the first chart displays the crime count on these historical events. The second chart displays crime counts over the months. The third chart displays the crime count over the days. You can change time range using date slider.

Crime and Population
This dashboard shows the relation between crime and population in different neighborhoods in Vancouver. We have four years(2003,2006,2011,2016) census data. Fist chart displays crime counts over different neighborhoods for four years. The second chart displays the per capita crime count over the neighborhood for four years. The third chart displays the population of neighborhoods for four years. This chart also presents the 15-55 population ratio which the ratio of population between 15-55 to whole population count.
