  2. Crime Distribution
Above charts shows crime distribution over four different factors. 1) Geographic location 2) Geographic location and neighborhoods 3) Time 4) Neighborhood.You can apply different filters to see more specific details. For example, you can select a range of date and types of crime. Crime distribution map shows the geographical distribution of crime incidents in Vancouver. You can zoom in to see a more specific region or zoom out to see overall distribution. You can hover the mouse pointer over crime incident point and it will show you more detailed information about that crime. Crime pie chart shows crime distribution over different neighborhoods. You can hover the mouse over different pie to get more detailed information. With Time-quantity chart you can visualize counts and types of crime over the continuous time period. Neibhour- quantity chart show counts and types of crime over different neighborhoods. Time-quantity and neighborhoods-quantity graphs are linked with each other. If you click any crime type for any specific neighborhood, its corresponding graph will be displayed in time-quantity chart.
